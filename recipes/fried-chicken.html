Fried Chicken Recipe

Description

Fried chicken is a crispy, golden-brown dish made by coating chicken pieces in a seasoned batter or flour mixture and deep-frying them until crunchy on the outside and juicy on the inside. This classic comfort food is loved worldwide, with variations like Southern fried chicken, Korean fried chicken, and buttermilk-fried chicken. The secret to perfect fried chicken lies in the seasoning, the crispy coating, and the frying technique. Ingredients

For The Chicken
  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks, or breasts)
  • 1 cup buttermilk (for marinating)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp paprika (optional)
For The Coating
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika (for extra flavor)
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for spice)
For Frying
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Steps to Make Fried Chicken

  1. In a bowl, mix chicken pieces, buttermilk, salt, pepper, and paprika. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for the best flavor).
  2. In a separate bowl, combine flour, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and cayenne (if using). This will give the chicken a crispy, flavorful crust.
  3. Remove the chicken from the buttermilk, letting excess drip off. Dredge each piece in the flour mixture, pressing to coat evenly. For extra crunch, dip it back into buttermilk and then coat in flour again.
